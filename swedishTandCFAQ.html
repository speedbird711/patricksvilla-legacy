Why should I book with you?

 A lot of people still book their holiday villa through a traditional travel agent. Quite often, you get your villa allocated upon arrival with no specifics as to the property you will be occupying and on which development it will be situated. Booking directly with us/the owners means you avoid this inconvenience and worry. You are able to ask us as many questions you need to beforehand, you can see the pictures on our website and know exactly what you are paying for.

Unlike so many other vacation homes, PatricksVilla and our guests are always being cared for by us, the owners. You don't have to deal with a management company, we are available 24/7.

When we bought PatricksVilla we made sure that it is a property that offers privacy. When you compare our pictures with pictures of other vacation homes you will find that a lot of them are either build in a long row, one next to each other, or directly opposite each other, overlooking the neighbors pool. You don't want to go for a swim in your own private pool only to find that the guy next door is just a few feet away, admiring your beautiful body.

    How easy is it to drive in Florida?

         Floridian roads are not too congested, compared to major cities. Obviously for some countries, the driver has to remember the cars are left hand drive.

    In Florida, unless stated, you may turn right at a red traffic light providing it is safe to do so and that you came to a full stop before proceeding.

    Headlights must be turned on during rain or reduced visibility.

    You are required to 'Stop' if a school bus stops, even if you are on the other side of the road.  It is illegal to overtake a school bus when it is stopped for loading or unloading of passengers.  You should remain stationary until all the children are clear of the road and the bus moves off. When entering a school district watch out for reduced speed signs and follow it.

    You may be required to pay in advance before filling up your car at most petrol stations. 

    If the police stop you for any reason you must stay in your vehicle until the officer approaches your vehicle.  Do not attempt to get out of the car and leave your hands visible on the steering wheel. You must have both your driving license and rental agreement with you.  Failure can result in a fine.
     
    You must report any accidents to the local police.  You must not leave the scene of an accident.  You must ask for a copy of the report for your car rental company.

    Never drink and drive. It is also illegal in the State of Florida to carry alcohol in the passenger area of the car or to have an opened container of alcohol in the vehicle. Always transport any alcoholic beverages in the boot/trunk of your car

    For more information on the Florida Highway Code please click on     www.hsmv.state.fl.us .
